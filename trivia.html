Trivia!

1. The first film HBO produced, under the name HBO Premiere Films (which became HBO Pictures in 1986) was The Terry Fox Story. It's HBO's third most aired movie of the '80s.

2. Sept. 5th, 1981 is the first continuous 24 hours of broadcast content, which became the norm for weekends. Until then, a typical day would last from about 5-6 pm to 12-1 am, with weekends starting sometime in the afternoon. Yes, HBO would sign off at the end of the night.

3. Dec 26, 1981 is when HBO first went 24 hours a day every day, including weekdays.

4. Sometimes HBO aired different versions of movies in the same month. Often they would offer separate dubbed or subtitled foreign films, like Fanny and Alexander, or different versions of films with different ratings, showing PG and R rated versions of Saturday Night Fever and Excalibur.

5. I believe Wed, July 12, 1989 is when HBO aired the most individual programs in one day in the '80s. 24 different programs aired over 24 hours, starting at midnight.

6. In 1984, a deal was struck between Paramount Pictures and HBO's competitor, Showtime, which gave them exclusive rights to many of their films for the next few years. There were notable exceptions, like Indiana Jones and the Temple of Doom, but many blockbusters never aired on HBO in the '80s.
